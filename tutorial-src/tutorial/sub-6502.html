MOS Technology 6502

The 6502 was an 8-bit computer processor that came out in 1975. Since it was cheap, had lots of features, and could shoot lasers if held the right way, it became very popular and kicked-started the microcomputer revolution.

It was the core of Steve Wozniak's original Apple computer, as well as the Atari, Commodore 64, and even the first game consoles from Nintendo.

While it was produced 40 years ago, it is still manufactured and used in select systems, even though most personal computers have moved on to larger, more powerful processors.
